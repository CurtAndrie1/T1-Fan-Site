DEMACIA

Demacia is a proud and noble kingdom founded on principles of justice, honor, and duty. Its citizens are known for their strong sense of morality and commitment to protecting the innocent. The kingdom is characterized by its grand castles, lush landscapes, and a well-organized military. Demacia values order and righteousness, often viewing magic with suspicion, especially if it threatens their ideals. The kingdom is known for its knights and champions who uphold justice and fight against tyranny. Notable champions from Demacia include Garen, a stalwart warrior and symbol of Demacian might; Lux, a powerful mage who uses her light-based magic to protect her homeland; and Jarvan IV, the prince of Demacia, known for his leadership and valor in battle.

BANDLE CITY

Bandle City is a whimsical and magical realm hidden within the forests of Runeterra. It is the home of the yordles, small, playful creatures who possess a strong affinity for magic and nature. The city is characterized by its vibrant colors, peculiar architecture, and an ever-changing landscape that reflects the imagination of its inhabitants. Bandle City is known for its carefree spirit, creativity, and connection to the spirit of the forest. The yordles often engage in playful antics and adventures, using their magic to protect their home from external threats. Notable champions from Bandle City include Teemo, the swift scout known for his guerrilla tactics and poisonous traps; Lulu, a whimsical mage who can manipulate the world around her and transform her allies; and Rumble, a clever inventor who pilots a mech to fight for his friends..

IONIA

Ionia is a mystical land filled with ancient traditions, spiritual practices, and a deep connection to nature. The kingdom is home to various martial arts and philosophies that emphasize harmony with the natural world. Ionia has faced external threats but remains resilient in preserving its culture. Ionia values peace, balance, and the protection of its natural surroundings. The people are often spiritual and practice various forms of martial arts that are deeply rooted in their beliefs. Notable champions from Ionia include Zed, a master of shadow manipulation and a skilled assassin; Kennen, a yordle ninja who harnesses the power of lightning; and Irelia, a skilled blade dancer who fights to protect her homeland.

NOXUS

TNoxus is a ruthless and expansionist empire that values strength and ambition above all else. It is characterized by its militaristic society, where the strong rise to power and the weak are often subjugated. The kingdom is known for its brutal conquests and the use of dark magic. Noxus is a land where power is the ultimate currency, and ambition is encouraged. Its citizens are often hardened warriors and cunning strategists who will do anything to achieve their goals. Notable champions from Noxus include Darius, a fearsome general known for his brutal strength and leadership; Swain, the cunning leader of Noxus, who uses dark magic to manipulate his enemies; and Katarina, a deadly assassin skilled in the art of combat and subterfuge.

PILTOVER

Piltover is a city of innovation and progress, known as the "City of Progress." It is a hub of technological advancement, where inventors and scientists push the boundaries of what is possible. The city thrives on trade and invention, making it a beacon of opportunity. Piltover is characterized by its towering spires, bustling markets, and a culture that celebrates ingenuity. The citizens are often driven by ambition and a desire to improve the world through technology. Notable champions from Piltover include Jayce, a brilliant inventor and warrior who wields a transforming hammer; Caitlyn, the skilled sheriff of Piltover, known for her marksmanship; and Heimerdinger, a genius yordle scientist who creates advanced technology and turrets.

ZAUN

Zaun is the underbelly of Piltover, characterized by pollution, crime, and a lack of regulation. It is a city where the poor and marginalized struggle to survive amidst the chaos. Despite its challenges, Zaun is a place of opportunity for those who are willing to take risks and push the boundaries of what is possible. Zaun values cunning and resourcefulness, and its citizens are often driven by a desire to survive and thrive in a harsh environment. Notable champions from Zaun include Janna, a skilled smuggler and wind mage; Twisted Fate, a cunning card shark and master of probability; and Dr. Mundo, a brilliant scientist who has dedicated his life to the pursuit of knowledge and power.

FRELJORD

The Freljord is a harsh and unforgiving land. Proud and fiercely independent, its people are born warriors, with a strong raiding culture. While there are many individual tribes within the Freljord, the battle lines are being drawn in a three-way civil war that will determine the future for them all. One tribe unflinchingly honors the traditions that have ensured its survival; another follows the dream of a united future, as foretold by a young idealist; while the third worships the power of an enigmatic sorceress.

BILGEWATER

Bilgewater is a haven for smugglers, marauders, and the unscrupulous, where fortunes are made and ambitions shattered in the blink of an eye. For those fleeing justice, debt, or persecution, it is a city of new beginnings; no one on the twisted streets of Bilgewater cares about your past. It's a melting pot of cultures, races, and creeds, alive with activity at all hours. While incredibly dangerous, Bilgewater is also ripe with opportunity, free from the shackles of government, regulation, and moral constraints. If you have the coin, almost anything can be purchased in Bilgewater, from outlawed hextech to the favor of local crime lords. Nevertheless, come daybreak, the unwary are found floating in the harbor, their purses empty and their throats slit.

SHADOW-ISLES

The land now known as the Shadow Isles was once a Blessed Isles Crest icon beautiful realm, but it was shattered by a magical cataclysm. Black Mist permanently shrouds the isles and the land itself is tainted, corrupted by malevolent sorcery sorcery. Living beings that stand upon the Shadow Isles slowly have their life-force leeched from them, which, in turn, draws the insatiable, predatory spirits of the dead. Those who perish within the Black Mist are condemned to haunt this melancholy land for eternity. Worse, the power of the Shadow Isles is waxing stronger with every passing year, allowing the shades of undeath to extend their range and reap souls all across Runeterra.

SHURIMA

The empire of Shurima was once a thriving civilization that spanned a vast desert. After an era of growth and prosperity, the fall of its gleaming capital left the empire in ruins. Over the millennia, tales of Shurima's glorious city became myth and religion among the descendants of the shattered survivors. Most of the nomadic inhabitants of Shurima search for basic sustenance in an unforgiving land. Some defend small outposts built around a few oasis. Others hunt buried riches among the ruins of the fallen empire, or obtain mercenary work, taking coin for their deeds before disappearing into the sands. Now, the tribes whisper from the heart of the desert: the city of Shurima has risen again.

TARGON

Like any place of myth, Mount Targon is a beacon to dreamers, madmen and questors of adventure. A mountainous and sparsely inhabited region to the west of Shurima Crest icon Shurima, Targon boasts the tallest peak in Runeterra. Located far from civilization, Mount Targon is all but impossible to reach, save by the most determined pilgrims, chasing some soul-deep yearning to reach its summit. Those hardy few who survive the arduous journey to the foot of the titanic mountain find a sky shimmering with celestial bodies; the sun and moons, but also constellations, planets, fiery comets that streak the darkness, and auspicious arrangements of stars. They return haunted and empty, or changed beyond all recognition. The people living at the mountain's base believe these to be aspects of long-vanished stellar beings, creatures powerful and ancient on a scale beyond human comprehension.[1] However, Mount Targon is simply a way into the celestial realm, and it would be wrong to attribute much in the way of mortal sensibilities, morals or concerns to what lies beyond the mountain.
